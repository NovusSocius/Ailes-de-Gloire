Armaments

The Eurofighter Typhoon is a multi-role fighter aircraft that can carry up to six bombs and six missiles. It has a 27 mm Mauser cannon and can carry a mix of missiles and bombs, including:

  • AIM-120B AMRAAM or Meteor FMRAAM
  • ASRAAM or IRIS-T air-to-air missiles
  • ALARM anti-radar missiles
  • Penguin anti-ship missiles
  • Brimstone anti-armor missiles
  • Paveway III laser guided bombs

History

The Eurofighter Typhoon is a twin-engine, canard-delta wing, multirole fighter aircraft. It was designed and built by a consortium of European aerospace companies. The development of the Eurofighter Typhoon began in the 1980s, with the first prototype flying in 1994. It entered service in 2003 and has since become one of the most advanced and capable fighter aircraft in the world.
